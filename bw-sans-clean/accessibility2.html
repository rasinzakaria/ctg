Accessibility Policy.

Updated March 2026  |  Effective Date: March 2026

This document consolidates CTG’s Integrated Accessibility Standards Policy and Customer Service Standard Policy. It is intended for publication on CTG’s website and is available in accessible formats upon request. To request this document in an alternate accessible format, please contact customerservice@ctgbrands.com.

1. Purpose

This document sets out CTG Brands Inc. (“CTG”, “we”, “our” or “us”) commitment to accessibility under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act, 2005 (“AODA”), including Ontario Regulation 191/11 (Integrated Accessibility Standards Regulation, or “IASR”) and the customer service requirements under Ontario law. It is intended to support equal access to our goods, services, facilities, employment opportunities and public-facing information for persons with disabilities.

Nothing in this document replaces or limits CTG’s obligations under the Ontario Human Rights Code or other applicable laws. Where there is a conflict between this policy and applicable law, the law prevails.

2. Scope

This policy applies to CTG’s Ontario operations and to all employees, officers, managers, temporary staff, contractors, agents and other persons who interact with the public, customers, applicants, employees or third parties on CTG’s behalf, to the extent applicable to their role.

This policy also applies to CTG’s public-facing websites and web content that CTG controls directly or through contractual arrangements, including third-party vendors engaged to provide digital services or content on CTG’s behalf.

3. Statement of Commitment

CTG is committed to identifying, removing and preventing barriers to accessibility and to meeting the accessibility needs of persons with disabilities in a timely manner. CTG will provide goods, services and facilities in a way that respects dignity and independence, supports integration where possible, and provides equal opportunity.

CTG is also committed to:

  • maintaining accessible employment practices;
  • providing accessible formats and communication supports where required;
  • ensuring that its public-facing internet websites and web content conform to WCAG 2.0 Level AA where required by law, subject to any applicable legal exceptions; and
  • involving persons with disabilities in the development, review and improvement of its accessibility policies, plans and practices where practicable.

4. Definitions

  • Accessible formats: may include, without limitation, large print, recorded audio, electronic formats, braille and other formats usable by persons with disabilities.
  • Barrier: means anything that prevents a person with a disability from fully participating in all aspects of society, including a physical, architectural, information or communications, attitudinal, technological, policy or practice barrier.
  • Communication supports: may include, without limitation, captioning, alternative and augmentative communication supports, plain-language summaries, sign language and other supports that facilitate effective communication.
  • Disability: has the meaning given in the AODA and the Ontario Human Rights Code and includes visible and non-visible disabilities.
  • IASR: means Ontario Regulation 191/11 (Integrated Accessibility Standards Regulation) made under the AODA.
  • Service animal: has the meaning recognized under applicable Ontario accessibility law.
  • Support person: means a person who accompanies a person with a disability in order to help with communication, mobility, personal care, medical needs or access to goods, services or facilities.
  • WCAG 2.0: means the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines, version 2.0, published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), which is the applicable web accessibility standard under the IASR.

5. Integrated Accessibility Standards

5.1 Accessibility policies and multi-year plan

CTG has developed, implemented and will maintain and review this accessibility policy and a multi-year accessibility plan as required by the IASR. CTG will post these documents on its website and provide them in an accessible format upon request.

CTG’s multi-year accessibility plan sets out the specific steps CTG will take to prevent and remove barriers and to meet its AODA obligations. The plan will be reviewed and updated at least once every five years, or more frequently where appropriate. The current multi-year plan is available on CTG’s website and in accessible formats upon request.

5.2 Training

CTG will provide training on the requirements of the IASR and on the Ontario Human Rights Code as it relates to persons with disabilities. Training will be provided as soon as practicable to all persons covered by this policy and whenever changes are made to CTG’s accessibility policies or practices. Training will be appropriate to the duties of each person’s role.

CTG will maintain a record of the training provided under this policy, including the dates on which training was provided and the number of individuals trained, as required by the IASR.

5.3 Information and communications

Upon request, CTG will provide or arrange for the provision of accessible formats and communication supports for persons with disabilities in a timely manner and at a cost that is no more than the regular cost charged to other persons, if any. CTG will consult with the requesting person in determining the suitability of an accessible format or communication support.

Where CTG prepares emergency procedures, plans or public safety information and makes that information available to the public, CTG will provide the information in an accessible format or with appropriate communication supports, upon request, as soon as practicable.

CTG maintains a publicly available process for receiving and responding to feedback and accessibility requests. Feedback may be submitted by email to customerservice@ctgbrands.com or by any other method made available by CTG. CTG will acknowledge and respond to feedback in a timely manner.

5.4 Websites and digital content (WCAG 2.0 Level AA)

CTG is committed to making its public-facing internet websites and web content accessible in accordance with the AODA and applicable standards. This commitment applies to all new and significantly refreshed internet websites and web content that CTG controls directly or through contractual arrangements.

As required by the IASR, CTG’s public-facing internet websites and web content shall conform to WCAG 2.0 Level AA, subject to statutory exceptions. CTG’s obligations under the IASR apply as follows:

  • All new public-facing internet websites and web content under CTG’s control must meet WCAG 2.0 Level AA (except where technically not feasible or as otherwise permitted by law).
  • Existing public-facing internet websites are required to meet WCAG 2.0 Level AA in accordance with the applicable compliance timelines under the IASR.
  • CTG may rely on third-party service providers for certain digital services; however, CTG will address accessibility obligations through governance, remediation and procurement practices, including incorporating accessibility requirements into vendor agreements where appropriate.

CTG will conduct periodic reviews of its website and digital content for accessibility and will take reasonable steps to remediate identified gaps. If you encounter accessibility barriers on CTG’s website, please contact us at customerservice@ctgbrands.com.

5.5 Employment

CTG will notify job applicants and the public about the availability of accommodations during its recruitment processes. This notice will be included in job postings and recruitment-related communications. Upon request, CTG will provide or arrange for suitable accommodations for applicants with disabilities during the recruitment, assessment and selection processes.

CTG will:

  • notify successful applicants of its policies for accommodating employees with disabilities;
  • inform employees about policies supporting employees with disabilities, including workplace accommodation policies;
  • develop, maintain and document individualized accommodation plans for employees with disabilities where required by law;
  • maintain return-to-work processes for employees who are absent due to a disability and require disability-related accommodations;
  • take accessibility needs and accommodation plans into account in performance management, career development, advancement and redeployment processes.

5.6 Individualized workplace emergency response information

CTG will provide individualized workplace emergency response information to employees who have a disability that requires such information, where CTG is aware of the need for accommodation. With the employee’s written consent, CTG may share this information with the person designated to assist the employee in an emergency.

CTG will review and, where necessary, update individualized emergency response information when an employee moves to a different location within CTG, when overall accommodation needs or plans are reviewed, and when CTG reviews its general emergency response policies.

5.7 Confidentiality

Information relating to a person’s disability or accommodation needs will be treated as confidential and will only be used, disclosed and retained as reasonably necessary for the accommodation process, workplace safety, legal compliance and related legitimate business purposes, subject to applicable law and CTG’s privacy practices.

6. Customer Service Standard

6.1 Provision of goods, services and facilities

CTG will use reasonable efforts to ensure that its goods, services and facilities are provided in a manner that respects the dignity and independence of persons with disabilities. CTG will seek to integrate accessibility into customer interactions and will provide alternative measures where reasonably necessary to enable access.

6.2 Assistive devices

Persons with disabilities may use their own assistive devices when accessing CTG’s goods, services or facilities, unless prohibited by law. Where a person with a disability encounters a barrier, CTG will work with the person to identify reasonable alternative measures.

6.3 Communication

When communicating with persons with disabilities, CTG will take into account the person’s disability and communication needs and will provide customer service in a manner that is clear, respectful and responsive. CTG will make reasonable efforts to communicate in a way that meets the individual’s needs.

6.4 Service animals

Persons with disabilities accompanied by a service animal are welcome in the parts of CTG’s premises open to the public or other third parties, unless the animal is otherwise excluded by law. If a service animal is excluded by law from a part of CTG’s premises, CTG will discuss other available measures to enable access by the person with a disability.

6.5 Support persons

A person with a disability who is accompanied by a support person may enter CTG’s premises with that support person. If CTG charges any fee in connection with a support person’s presence (for example, for admission to an event), CTG will provide advance notice of the amount, if any, as required by law.

6.6 Notice of temporary disruptions

If there is a planned or unexpected disruption to facilities or services that persons with disabilities rely upon to access CTG’s goods, services or facilities, CTG will provide notice as soon as reasonably practicable. The notice will include, where available: the reason for the disruption, its anticipated duration, and a description of alternative facilities or services, if any. Notices will be posted at the point of disruption and in such other locations as are reasonable in the circumstances.

6.7 Feedback process

CTG welcomes feedback regarding accessibility and the manner in which CTG provides goods, services or facilities to persons with disabilities. Feedback may be submitted by:

  • Email: customerservice@ctgbrands.com
  • Mail: CTG Brands Inc., 123 Great Gulf Drive, Vaughan, Ontario, Canada

CTG will acknowledge receipt of feedback and respond within a reasonable time. Where feedback indicates an accessibility barrier, CTG will review the matter and take reasonable steps to address it. A summary of feedback received may inform updates to CTG’s multi-year accessibility plan.

6.8 Availability of this policy and related documents

This policy, CTG’s multi-year accessibility plan and related documents required by law will be made available on CTG’s website and will be provided in accessible formats upon request, at no additional cost. To request an accessible format, please contact customerservice@ctgbrands.com.

7. Administration and Review

Questions about this policy, requests for accessible formats or communication supports, and accessibility feedback may be directed to:

  • Email: customerservice@ctgbrands.com
  • Mail: CTG Brands Inc., 123 Great Gulf Drive, Vaughan, Ontario, Canada

CTG will designate responsibility for implementing this policy and monitoring AODA compliance. CTG may amend this policy from time to time to reflect operational changes, legal developments or accessibility improvements. The most current version will be posted on CTG’s website with the applicable effective date.

This policy will be reviewed at least every five years, or more frequently as required by law or operational need.
