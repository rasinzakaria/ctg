Privacy Policy.

Updated March 2026  |  Effective Date: March 2026

This policy describes how CTG Brands Inc. collects, uses, discloses, protects and retains personal information through our website, customer accounts, sales activities and related communications. To request this document in an accessible format, please contact customerservice@ctgbrands.com.

1. Scope and Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how CTG Brands Inc. (“CTG”, “we”, “our” or “us”) collects, uses, discloses, protects and retains personal information through our website, customer accounts, sales activities and related communications. It also explains the choices available to individuals regarding their information.

This Privacy Policy applies to information collected through www.ctgbrands.com and other CTG-controlled online services that link to or reference this Privacy Policy. It should be read together with CTG’s Terms of Use and Accessibility Policy, each available on our website.

In this Privacy Policy, “personal information” means information about an identifiable individual, as defined under applicable Canadian privacy law (including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (“PIPEDA”) and applicable provincial legislation). Business contact information used solely to communicate with an individual in relation to their employment, business or profession may not be treated as personal information in all circumstances under applicable law.

2. Accountability and Contact

CTG is responsible for personal information under its control and has designated a person or persons to be accountable for CTG’s compliance with its privacy obligations. Questions, requests or complaints regarding this Privacy Policy or CTG’s privacy practices may be directed to:

  • Email: customerservice@ctgbrands.com
  • Mail: CTG Brands Inc., 123 Great Gulf Drive, Vaughan, Ontario, Canada

CTG will acknowledge and respond to privacy inquiries and complaints in a timely manner. Where a complaint is found to be justified, CTG will take appropriate steps to resolve it.

3. Information We Collect

CTG collects personal information only as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy. We collect the following categories of information:

  • Information you provide directly. For example: your name, title, company name, billing and shipping address, email address, telephone number, account credentials, order information, correspondence, preferences and any other information you choose to provide.
  • Transaction and account information. For example: order history, wholesale account details, payment-related information handled through our payment service providers, returns, credits and account administration records.
  • Technical and usage information. For example: IP address, browser type, device identifiers, operating system, pages visited, dates and times of access, referring URLs, approximate location derived from IP address, and website interaction data collected through cookies or similar technologies.
  • Information from service providers or business partners. For example: shipping status updates, payment confirmations, fraud screening results and marketing analytics data.

CTG does not intentionally collect sensitive personal information (such as health information or financial account numbers) through its website. Please do not submit such information unless specifically requested.

4. How We Use Personal Information

CTG uses personal information only for purposes that a reasonable person would consider appropriate in the circumstances and that are consistent with the purposes identified at the time of collection. These purposes include:

  • Provide products and services. To create and administer accounts, process orders, arrange shipping, manage returns, provide customer support and communicate about transactions.
  • Operate and improve our business. To maintain our website, troubleshoot issues, prevent fraud, enforce our terms, conduct analytics and improve our products, services and user experience.
  • Marketing. To send promotional or product-related communications where permitted by law. You may unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time using the unsubscribe mechanism in the message or by contacting us at customerservice@ctgbrands.com.
  • Legal and compliance purposes. To meet legal, regulatory, accounting, tax, audit and record-keeping requirements and to establish, exercise or defend legal claims.

5. Consent

Where required by applicable law, CTG obtains consent for the collection, use and disclosure of personal information. Depending on the circumstances and sensitivity of the information, consent may be express or implied. By providing information to CTG, using our website, creating an account, placing an order or otherwise interacting with us, you consent to the collection, use and disclosure of your information as described in this Privacy Policy, except where the law requires another form of consent.

You may withdraw consent at any time, subject to legal or contractual restrictions and reasonable notice, by contacting us at customerservice@ctgbrands.com. Please note that withdrawal of consent may affect CTG’s ability to provide certain products, services or account features.

Where CTG relies on implied consent (for example, for transactional communications), you may opt out at any time. CTG will honour opt-out requests promptly and in accordance with applicable law, including Canada’s Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL).

6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

CTG and its service providers may use cookies, pixels, web beacons, server logs and similar technologies to operate our website, remember user preferences, analyze traffic, improve functionality, support security and measure the effectiveness of marketing.

You can control certain cookies through your browser settings or other tools that may be available on our website. Disabling cookies may affect the availability or functionality of some website features. CTG’s use of cookies is subject to this Privacy Policy and any separate cookie notice posted on our website.

CTG does not currently respond to “Do Not Track” signals from web browsers, as no industry standard for such signals has been established in Canada. If this practice changes, CTG will update this Privacy Policy accordingly.

7. Disclosure of Personal Information

CTG does not sell personal information. CTG may disclose personal information in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers. To third parties that perform services on CTG’s behalf, such as website hosting, payment processing, shipping, analytics, customer relationship management, marketing support and information technology services. CTG requires service providers to protect personal information in a manner consistent with CTG’s privacy obligations.
  • Corporate and commercial transactions. In connection with a proposed or completed merger, financing, acquisition, reorganization, sale of assets or similar transaction, subject to customary confidentiality protections where appropriate.
  • Legal reasons. Where disclosure is required or permitted by law, court order, subpoena or other lawful authority, or where reasonably necessary to protect CTG’s rights, property or safety, or the rights, property or safety of others.
  • With your direction or consent. Where you ask us to disclose information or otherwise provide consent to disclosure.

Where CTG transfers personal information to service providers located outside Canada (including in the United States), CTG will take contractual or other measures to provide comparable privacy protection, recognizing that such information may be subject to the laws of the jurisdiction where it is processed.

8. Safeguards

CTG uses administrative, technical and physical safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the personal information in its custody or control. These measures are designed to protect against unauthorized access, collection, use, disclosure, copying, modification, disposal or destruction.

No method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure, and CTG cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a privacy breach that poses a real risk of significant harm to individuals, CTG will notify affected individuals and the applicable regulatory authority as required by law.

9. Retention

CTG retains personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, to support legitimate business needs, and to comply with legal, accounting, tax, dispute-resolution and record-retention requirements. When personal information is no longer required, CTG will securely delete, destroy or anonymize it, as appropriate.

10. Access, Correction and Requests

Subject to applicable law and verification of identity, an individual may request access to personal information that CTG holds about them and may request corrections if the information is inaccurate or incomplete. CTG will respond to access and correction requests within 30 days, or such other period as may be permitted or required by law.

Requests may be sent to customerservice@ctgbrands.com. CTG may need to request additional information to verify identity before responding. In certain circumstances permitted by law, CTG may decline to provide access to personal information, in which case CTG will advise the individual of the reason, subject to any legal restrictions on disclosure of that reason.

11. Third-Party Sites and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, tools or services for convenience only. CTG is not responsible for the privacy practices, content or security of those third parties. Individuals should review the privacy terms of any third-party site or service they use.

Please remember that when you use a link to go from our Site to another web site, our Privacy Policy is no longer in effect. Your browsing and interaction on any other web site, including web sites that have a link on our Site, is subject to that web site’s own rules and policies. Please read those rules and policies before proceeding.

12. Children’s Privacy

CTG’s website and services are not directed to children under the age of 13, and CTG does not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 13 through the website without appropriate parental or guardian consent where required by law. If CTG becomes aware that personal information has been collected from a child under 13 without appropriate consent, CTG will take steps to delete that information.

13. Accessibility of this Policy

This Privacy Policy is published on CTG’s website and is intended to be accessible in accordance with CTG’s obligations under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act, 2005 (AODA). If you require this Privacy Policy in an accessible format or with communication supports, please contact us at customerservice@ctgbrands.com and CTG will provide the document in a suitable accessible format at no additional cost.

14. Changes to this Privacy Policy

CTG may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted on our website with a revised effective date. Where required by law, CTG will take additional steps to notify individuals of, or obtain consent to, material changes.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how CTG protects your personal information.

Your Consent

By using our Site you consent to our collection and use of your Personal Information, Use Information and all other information as described in this Privacy Policy. If we change our Privacy Policy, we will post those changes on our Site to keep you aware of what information we collect, how we use it and under what circumstances we may disclose it.

Our Privacy Policy may be amended at any time by us from time to time without specific notice to you. The latest Privacy Policy will be posted on the Site, and you should review this Agreement prior to using the Site.
